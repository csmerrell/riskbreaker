# Factions

## Riskbreakers / Minstrels
These aren't actually factions. They're just independent actors with loose definitions.

A Riskbreaker is a name casually given to anyone who braves the darkness to make an area safer for others. It's typically assigned locally to individuals whose actions earn storied respect within their home.

Minstrels are often the individuals who knight someone an riskbreaker: When a song is first sung about your deeds, that is typically the first makings of a riskbreaker's story.

Minstrels make many of the world's firesides, spreading comfort by turning the shadows behind a fire's light into symbols of levity and entertainment. 
- "Turn shadow into solace."
    - The minstrel's promise
- Contrast is the tool a minstrel uses to chip away at fear. 

## Scholars of the Final Star (Astrologians & Ascetics)
Astrologians divine the aether of the air, while Ascetics breathe it in.

The flame of an Ascetic's scarf can sometimes be seen warping the air at its tips. Stories speak of ascetics who vanish from sight, only to appear again in some unrelated place. Those who walk between planes never speak of the experience, so it is unclear what or how they achieve this.

The vocal scholars of the star resent what they perceive as secrecy. To capture the night's weave is their grand mission, and they live constantly vexed by this knowledge that eludes them. Some have tried to adopt the ascetic's habit to break the silence, but none have succeeded.

The ascetics, however, don't consider their silence a secrecy of any kind. Where others see the fiery muffler as a sheath, the ascetic sees it as an open mouth: a tongue of flames sharing the warmth of the star in local manifestiation.

## Mist Wisps < Moonfang & Night Howler || Shadeweaver
Occasionally, ethereal white wisps can be seen stalking the blackened night. Reliable sightings date to the first appearance of the Necrohol, but they were incredibly rare until recently. 

What's worse, the recent claims indicate the first time the wisps have shown hostility, with a handful of incidents reporting damaged property and two missing persons.

Every appearance since the two missing persons were reported, all wisp sightings have been accompanied by chains of howling that travel town to town.

Some have attempted to follow the howls to find a source, only to be met not by white wisps, but shades crawling in the night with color blacker still.

Such perfect black had only been seen previously in rifts cutting open the necrohol, but now they seem to have taken shape and move among us.

The common folk scare says the two who went missing weren't taken by white wisps at all. People worry the necrohol is advancing and taking motion, and that if you don't watch your step at night, you may mistakenly step into a rift that had crept beneath your feet.

Only sealbinders and scholars of the first star have survived enounters with a rift into the necrohol, and even those are deeply limited.

Sealbinders interact only with their glaives. Scholars have only managed to poke their heads in after binding themselves to magicked tomes here. Both utilize binding techniques to tether a thing here in this plane, lest it get sucked up in a current that spirits them away into the Necrohol forever.

## Stonecaller & Naturalists
The stonecaller sits deliberately outside of any known factions. Their scaleborn race is not recognized or known among the inhabitants of Thurala, though their powers are.

The stonecaller is initially confused by Thurala's geology and flora, but in time their attunement with the earth grows and they commune their way to the Naturalists' enclave.

One more than one occasion, people have mistakenly called the Stonecaller a "flameeater," wondering if their arrival in thurala implies a long-lost myth about people who wed dragons and produced bipedal dragonkin.

Such an evolution would be ridiculous, as the species are wholly incompatible. The stonecaller's actual ancestry dates back so far from common ancestry with dragons that even calling them even distant cousins would be a stretch.

The Naturalists are aware of the failing stars in the night sky, but they're also thoroughly perplexed by it. They've been speaking with the trees out of concern that the star feeding them in the neighboring plane might also have disappeared, but the trees assure them that they are fine.

The Naturalists' more immediate concern is the planets moons. They have been charting minor anomalies in the tide, but haven't been able to detect any noteworthy changes in the lunar calendar.

It is through the Naturalists that the Stonecaller eventually gains passage to the spring plane through the aether tree.

## Eyes of the Necrohol < Sealbinders (Bladesealers & Lifebinders)

Rifts into the necrohol are normal enough that a new rupture hardly draws attention. Rifts open. Sometimes a fresh new horror crawls out. The local riskbreakers hold it off until people can retreat and life continues.

But life is never the same, unless the sealbinders eventually arrive.

There are eyes inside the necrohol. Eyes that look out and study us. Eyes that belong to someone or something we do not know.

History says we lost some fair brave souls who stepped into the rifts to see what watched us from that unknown plane. Their loss is what prompted the arts of sealbinding. It is the art of tethering the soul through anchors.

Without an anchor, there is no path back from the Necrohol. The complexity of anchors needed to forcibly close a rift is beyond the art's most common practitioners. But the capabilities of the sealbinder don't begin and end with the rifts.

Bladesealers engrave anchors to their weapons, allowing them to isolate some fraction of their opponent and tether their soul toward some variety of effect.

Lifebinders anchor themselves directly to others, creating links to send and draw health through the aether.

Neither practitioner is particularly well regarded. For every sealbinder who has restored a town to its former comfort with the closure of a malignant rift. For every lifebinder who has expended their own vitality to heal an ailing child, and every bladesealer that has cut down one of those rift's horrors.

For every good binder, three more have abused their art.

We do not know when a sealbinder reveals themself, whether they will save us or siphon us. We cannot know, so we do not seek them when the eyes of the necrohol open at our backs.

The eyes of the necrohol have never hurt us. They have only observered.

## Soulswords >><< Spellblades
A Soulsword is just a Lifebinder's skill used with a Bladesealer's flair. They carve anchors into a wooden handle, use it to circulate energy through their body and ignite something that looks like a sword in ethereal flames.

But a Soulsword has never been brandished against an innocent.

No one really knows why "Soulsword = Safe" and "Bladesealer = Not," but it's true. If someone whips out a fiery sword in public, nobody hides. Nobody needs to.

Maybe it stems from the fact that Soulswords all began as members of the Matron's Spellblades. Maybe its just dumb luck that the soulswords' seven holders in all of history happened to be paragons of human virtue.

Maybe both facts simply speak to how demanding it is to learn an art whose power doesn't reflect the overwhelming effort.

Scholars have never been given an answer how the Matron selects someone to don the robes of the Soulsword. Her Spellblades quickly offer up the secrets of their blade enchantments as a diversion. Much like a bladesealer's weapon, a spellblade carves designs into their weapons. They call them runes, not anchors, though.

Where a sealbinder's anchors move energies between local vicinity living beings, a spellblade draws its magicks from the Matron herself.

## Bear && Night Howlers
The player can be cursed to turn into a bear. If they do, their access to breakthroughs is limited (and refunded).

Advancing the bear's capacity for lunar, naturalist, and sealbinding magicks will advance the magick capacities of various enemy types who reap the windfall.

Bears gain a special affinity with night howlers, resulting in advantageous moonfang synergy.
